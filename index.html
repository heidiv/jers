Bio

Dr. Jeremy Strachan recently completed his doctorate in Musicology from the University of Toronto (2015). His thesis, “Music, Communications, Place: Udo Kasemets and Experimentalism in 1960s Toronto” examines the beginning of experimental music practice in postwar Toronto. Dr. Strachan has published on topics ranging from modernism in North America, to experimental music and cultural geography, to improvisation and avant-garde jazz. He is a recipient of the AMS-50 Dissertation Fellowship (2013-2014), and the SOCAN/George Proctor Prize (2011). Dr. Strachan’s current research is focused on the historiography of experimental music.
